0. Mantra Meditation (Body-Brahma)

1

Sit in padmasana (lotus position) or sukhasana (easy pose) in a quiet, dimly lit space.

2

Alternate Nostril Breathing:

Inhale deeply through your left nostril while counting to 22, then exhale through your right nostril while counting to 11.

3

Now inhale through your right nostril while counting to 22, then exhale through your left nostril while counting to 11.

4

Repeat steps 2 and 3 for five complete cycles.

5
Hare Krishna Hare Krishna
Krishna Krishna Hare Hare
Hare Rama Hare Rama
Rama Rama Hare Hare

Or chant "Om Namah Shivaya" or any personal deity's name that resonates with you. Chant aloud so you can hear yourself, counting repetitions with your fingers (ideally 108 times or as many as possible).

6

While chanting, focus your mind on your breath. Visualize your chosen deity residing within your body.

7

Notice any areas of discomfort or pain that may distract you from your chanting. Gently bring your awareness to these areas, then return your focus to your breath.

8

Continue this practice until your mind becomes calm. Then offer gratitude to the divine before leaving your seated position.

Pro Tip: Practicing regularly at the same time and place yields the best results.
For Sleep: When using this technique for sleep, stop here. Continuing further may disrupt sleepiness.

2. Mind - Vishnu (Observer Consciousness)

"As Krishna remained detached in action, observe your challenges without attachment"
1

Reflect on how Lord Krishna solved problems in the Mahabharata:

  • His maintenance of dharma in difficult situations
  • Bhagavad Gita's wisdom on detached action
  • Strategic thinking without emotional interference
2

Identify and release these six mental obstacles (Ari Shad Varga):

  1. Kama (excessive desires)
  2. Krodha (anger)
  3. Lobha (greed)
  4. Moha (attachment)
  5. Mada (pride)
  6. Matsarya (jealousy)
Practice Benefit: Daily observation with detachment leads to clearer decision-making aligned with your higher purpose.

3. Soul - Maheshwar (Pure Consciousness)

"Like Shiva in samadhi, become one with your highest purpose"
1

Meditate on these aspects of Shiva:

  • Determination: Like drinking the poison to save the world
  • Beyond Gunas: Transcending sattva, rajas and tamas
  • Indestructibility: The eternal nature of consciousness
2

Activate your Shiva-state:

  1. Fix goals with unwavering focus (like Shiva's third eye)
  2. Act with complete commitment (like Shiva's cosmic dance)
  3. Remain undisturbed by outcomes (like Shiva covered in ashes)
Soul Benefit: Regular practice connects you to your indomitable spiritual core for transformative action.
